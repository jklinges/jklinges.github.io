Wind Speed (mph) vs. Latitude

Wind is created when air moves from areas of high pressure toward areas where the air pressure is low. Seasonal temperature changes and the Earth's rotation also affect wind speed and direction. While wind speed appears to slightly increase as we move away from the equator, there is no strong association between latitude and wind speed. There are a few cities in the Northern Hemisphere that seem to experience high windspeeds (25 mph to 35 mph). However, to conclusively describe a pattern, we would need to go beyond the existing range in data to reach cities which are closer to the poles.
